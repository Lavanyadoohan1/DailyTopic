Gaspard Ulliel, the star of Marvel’s ‘Moon Knight’ series, died at the age of 37 in a skiing accident.

ByAdmin

Jan 20, 2022

Gaspard Ulliel, one of France’s most celebrated actors and a star of Marvel’s upcoming ‘Moon Knight’ series, died on Wednesday after a skiing accident, according to news agency AFP. He was 37 years old.

The two-time Cesar Award winner was hospitalized on Tuesday after suffering a head injury and did not recover, according to the actor’s family and local agent. He was involved in a collision on the slopes of the Savoie region.

“Gaspard Ulliel grew up with cinema, and cinema grew with him,” French Prime Minister Jean Castex said in a heartfelt tribute to the late actor. They were madly in love with each other. We will miss seeing his most beautiful interpretations with a heavy heart. We have a French actor who has passed away.”

On social media, people are paying their respects to Gaspard Ulliel. Filmmaker Peter Webber, who directed Ulliel in Hannibal Rising, French director and actor Xavier Dolan, who cast the actor as the lead in his It’s Only the End of the World, and French actors Jean Dujardin, Juliette Binoche, and Anthony Delon, among others, all expressed their condolences on the late actor’s death.

View this post on Instagram

A post shared by Xavier Dolan (@xavierdolan)

Ulliel played Midnight Man in ‘Moon Knight,’ but he’s best known for his role in Xavier Dolan’s Canadian-French film ‘It’s Only the End of the World,’ alongside Marion Cotillard, Lea Seydoux, and Vincent Cassel. The film won the Grand Prix at Cannes as well as three Cesars (French Oscars).

In Bertrand Bonello’s biopic ‘Saint Laurent,’ Ulliel also gave a powerful performance as the famous French fashion designer Yves Saint Laurent.

France’s Culture Minister, Roselyne Bachelot, described Ulliel’s death as “so unfair,” saying: “Such a brilliant actor who was reaped by the sickle of death at the peak of his career.”

He was present in our lives for nearly 20 years with magnificent performances, winning his first Cesar for the best male newcomer with ‘A Very Long Engagement.’

The Cannes Film Festival’s Director, Thierry Fremaux, also expressed his condolences. According to ‘Variety,’ Ulliel was a regular on the red carpet in Cannes, where the actor’s films were frequently premiered.

“Gaspard belonged to this new generation of actors who were shaping French cinema for the future.” “He knew how to choose his roles and shape his career in a way that fulfilled every promise,” Fremaux told ‘Variety.’
