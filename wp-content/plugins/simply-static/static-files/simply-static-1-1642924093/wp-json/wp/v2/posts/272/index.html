    Reliance Jio, India’s largest telecom company, announced its financial results for the period October to December 2021, revealing strong momentum in the amount it earns on average from each subscriber, also known as ARPU (average revenue per user).<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Between October and December 2021, Reliance Jio’s earnings before interest, tax, depreciation, and amortization (EBITDA) increased by 18%, far exceeding analyst expectations of 4-5% growth.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Surprisingly, this is Jio’s highest ARPU to date, surpassing the December 2020 figure. This is after accounting for the change in the IUC (interconnect usage charges) regime, which went into effect in January 2021 and resulted in a drop in ARPU numbers for all telecom companies.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    After ignoring analyst warnings for several months, Jio finally raised tariffs after rivals Airtel and Vodafone Idea announced 20-25 percent hikes. Jio’s tariffs remain the lowest of the three, and JP Morgan analysts expect the benefits of this increase to be realized only in the June 2022 quarter.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
