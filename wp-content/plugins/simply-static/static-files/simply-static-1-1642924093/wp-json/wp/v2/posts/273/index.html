Nature has a few tricks up her sleeve. It can catch you off guard at any time. One such occasion occurred recently in the United States, when dozens of bizarre sand sculptures were spotted near Lake Michigan.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

What makes these’sand hoodoos’, which dotted the shoreline, unique is that they were formed by a combination of strong wind, frozen moisture in the sand, and erosion.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The term “sand hoodoos” refers to naturally formed, thin rock spires that are typically found in canyons.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

These sculptures could stand up to 15 inches tall. They were discovered in the area, which is approximately 82 miles from Grand Rapids.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Sand hoodoos have a short lifespan because they collapse within hours as the weather warms throughout the day.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Visitors to the Lake Michigan beach near St Joseph, Michigan, were in for a treat when they saw the unusual sight.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

“It was like being on another planet.” Terri Abbott told Fox Weather, “I’ve never seen anything like them, and I spend a lot of time there.”<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Abbott had seen them in Tescornia Park in the city of southwestern Michigan.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

“I believe what happened is that the sand became frozen. Then, a few weeks ago, we had a major storm with high winds pass through. “The wind blew along the beach, blasting out the areas that were less frozen,” said Alan Arbogast, Geology Chairman at Michigan State University.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
