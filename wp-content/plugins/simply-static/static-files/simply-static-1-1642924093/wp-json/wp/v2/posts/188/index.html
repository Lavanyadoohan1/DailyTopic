The price and availability of the Xiaomi 11T Pro in India have been announced. The device is Xiaomi’s new premium smartphone in India, competing with the OnePlus 9RT. The Xiaomi 11T Pro will be available for purchase beginning at 2 p.m. on January 19 as part of Amazon’s Great Republic Day Sale.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

There are three storage options for the Xiaomi 11T Pro. The phone’s base model has 8GB of RAM and 128GB of storage. The price is Rs 39,999. There is also an 8GB+ 256GB variant available for Rs 41,999. Finally, the top-tier 12GB + 256GB variant costs Rs 43,999. The phone is available in three colors: Meteorite Black, Moonlight White, and Celestial Magic.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Customers with a CITI Bank card can get a Rs 5000 discount on the Xiaomi 11T Pro during the sale as part of the launch offers.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The Xiaomi 11T Pro undercuts its archrival, the OnePlus 9RT, in terms of pricing (hands-on review). OnePlus released the 9RT with two storage options. The OnePlus 9RT price in India is Rs 42,999 for the 8GB + 128GB variant, and Rs 46,999 for the 12GB + 256GB variant.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The Xiaomi 11T Pro will be available for purchase on Amazon India, Mi Home Stores, and Mi.com beginning at 2 p.m. on January 19.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Specifications for the Xiaomi 11T Pro<\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

The display on the Xiaomi 11T Pro is 6.67-inch Full HD+ AMOLED with a 20:9 aspect ratio. The phone has a flat-screen with a hole-punch cutout in the top center for the front-facing 16MP camera. The screen has a peak brightness of 1000 nits and is HD10+ certified.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The phone is powered by a Qualcomm Snapdragon 888 SoC. It has 256GB of storage and up to 12GB of RAM. The Xiaomi 11T Pro comes with a 5,000 mAh battery that supports 120W Xiaomi HyperCharge fast charging. According to the manufacturer, the phone can be fully charged in 17 minutes. We’ll put the claim to the test in our upcoming Xiaomi 11T Pro review.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The Xiaomi 11T Pro camera module has a triple-sensor setup on the back. The primary sensor is a 108MP Samsung ISOCELL HM2 sensor, which is also found in the Xiaomi 11i HyperCharge (Review). The main camera lacks optical image stabilization (OIS), but Xiaomi claims that the camera on the 11T Pro captures more frames at multiple exposures and merges them to process the final output.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

In addition, the device has an 8MP ultrawide camera and a 5MP tele-macro camera. In terms of software, the Xiaomi smartphone comes pre-installed with Android 11-based MIUI 12.5. It has dual speakers tuned by Harmon Kardon with Dolby Atmos, which is also available on the flagship Mi 11 Ultra (Review)<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
