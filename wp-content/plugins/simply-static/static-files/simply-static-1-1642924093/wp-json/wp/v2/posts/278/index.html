The largest space telescope, the James Webb Space Telescope, has used all of its hexagonal mirror segments. The delicate operation of removing the individual mirror segments of the James Webb observatory from their launch positions took nearly a week. NASA stated that its engineers sent commands to the motors, known as actuators, to slowly move each segment about half the length of a paper clip in order to free the mirrors from their launch restraints and provide enough space for mirror alignment.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The James Webb space telescope will now begin its orbit around the sun. The telescope will be nearly one million miles from Earth. Erin Wolf, the mission’s program manager, also described how the mirrors on the James Webb observatory were moved and tested. He claimed that the motors had completed over a million revolutions this week. The team relocated all 132 actuators in the primary and secondary mirror segments. He explained that the actuators free the mirrors from their launch restraints and give each segment space to be adjusted later in other directions by using six motors that deploy each segment about half the length of a paper clip.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
